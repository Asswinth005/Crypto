What is Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is the world's first decentralized digital currency that allows peer-to-peer transactions without the need for intermediaries like banks.

How Bitcoin Works

Bitcoin operates on a technology called blockchain, which is a distributed ledger that records all transactions across a network of computers.

  • Decentralized: No central authority controls Bitcoin
  • Limited Supply: Only 21 million BTC will ever exist
  • Secure: Cryptography protects transactions
  • Transparent: All transactions are publicly visible

Why Invest in Bitcoin?

Bitcoin has emerged as a store of value and hedge against inflation, often referred to as "digital gold" by investors.

  • Store of Value: Limited supply makes it resistant to inflation
  • Global Accessibility: Available to anyone with internet access
  • Portable: Transfer large amounts anywhere in the world
  • Institutional Adoption: Growing acceptance by companies and funds

Bitcoin: The Future of Money

Since its creation in 2009, Bitcoin has grown from an experimental digital currency to a global financial asset with a market capitalization exceeding $1 trillion. It represents a fundamental shift in how we think about money, value, and trust.

Bitcoin transactions are irreversible and pseudonymous

What is Bitcoin mining?

Bitcoin mining is the process of validating transactions and adding them to the blockchain. Miners use powerful computers to solve complex mathematical problems, and are rewarded with new bitcoins for their work.
